# Protein Guidelines

## Daily Target

| Metric | Amount | Basis |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Minimum | 1.6 g/kg | General population maintenance |
| Optimal (muscle gain) | 1.6-2.2 g/kg | Evidence-based range |
| Aggressive (cutting) | 2.2-3.0 g/kg | Catabolic protection |
| Current user target | 140-170g/day | 2.2-2.7 g/kg at 63.4 kg |

## Timing

| Window | Amount | Rationale |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Pre-workout (1-2h before) | 20-30g | Amino acid availability |
| Post-workout (within 2h) | 30-40g | Recovery stimulus |
| Per meal | 30-40g | Max MPS stimulation per meal |
| Before bed | 20-30g casein | Slow-release overnight |

## Sources

### Animal (complete proteins)
- Chicken breast (31g per 100g)
- Beef (26g per 100g)
- Eggs (6g per egg)
- Greek yogurt (10g per 100g)
- Cottage cheese (11g per 100g)
- Milk (3.4g per 100ml)
- Whey protein (25g per scoop)

### Plant (incomplete, combine)
- Lentils (9g per 100g)
- Chickpeas (9g per 100g)
- Tofu (8g per 100g)
- Quinoa (4g per 100g)

## Key Points

- Protein synthesis maxes out at ~0.4g/kg per meal (~30-40g)
- Total daily intake matters more than timing
- Whey is fast-absorbing (post-workout optimal)
- Casein is slow-absorbing (before bed optimal)
- Spread protein across 4-5 meals for best results

## References

- Morton et al. (2018) — systematic review: 1.6 g/kg sufficient for most
- Schoenfeld et al. (2013) — post-exercise protein timing meta-analysis
- Moore et al. (2009) — 20g optimal per dose for young males
