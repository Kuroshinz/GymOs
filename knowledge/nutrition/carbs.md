# Carbohydrate Guidelines

## Role

- Primary fuel source for high-intensity training
- Glycogen stores directly impact performance
- Protein-sparing (adequate carbs = less protein used for energy)

## Daily Target

| Context | Amount | Notes |
|---------|--------|-------|
| Training days | 300-400g | Fuels PPL-UL sessions |
| Rest days | 200-300g | Lower activity, less need |
| Current user target | 300-400g | Lean bulk, active daily |

## Timing

| Window | Amount | Type | Purpose |
|--------|--------|------|---------|
| Pre-workout (1-2h) | 40-60g | Fast-digesting (white rice, bananas, oats) | Glycogen top-up |
| Intra-workout | Optional | Sports drink or carb powder | Extended sessions >90 min |
| Post-workout (within 2h) | 40-60g | Fast + moderate (rice, potatoes, fruit) | Glycogen replenishment |
| Other meals | Rest of target | Whole food sources (rice, oats, quinoa, potatoes, whole grains) | Sustained energy |

## Sources

### Fast-digesting (pre/post workout)
- White rice
- White potatoes
- Bananas
- White bread
- Sports drinks / carb powder
- Dates

### Slow-digesting (general meals)
- Oats
- Brown rice
- Quinoa
- Whole grain bread
- Sweet potatoes
- Legumes
- Vegetables

## Key Points

- Carbs are not the enemy on a bulk — they fuel performance
- Low carb = poor performance = less stimulus = less growth
- Fiber intake should be 30g+ regardless of carb source
- Simple carbs are useful around workouts
- Complex carbs for all other meals
- If calories need adjusting, adjust carbs first
