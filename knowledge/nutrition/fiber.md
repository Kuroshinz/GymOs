# Fiber Guidelines

## Role

- Digestive health and regularity
- Gut microbiome health (prebiotic effects)
- Satiety and appetite regulation
- Blood sugar stabilisation
- Cholesterol management

## Daily Target

| Metric | Amount | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Minimum (adults) | 25-30g | General health |
| Optimal | 30-40g | While bulking (high food intake) |
| Current user target | 30g+ | On 2800+ calorie bulk |

## Sources

### Soluble Fiber (blood sugar, cholesterol)
- Oats
- Apples
- Citrus fruits
- Carrots
- Beans and legumes
- Psyllium husk

### Insoluble Fiber (digestive regularity)
- Whole grains
- Vegetables (broccoli, leafy greens)
- Nuts and seeds
- Potatoes with skin
- Brown rice

## Practical Tips for 30g+

- Oatmeal breakfast: ~5g
- Apple or banana snack: ~3-4g
- Vegetables with lunch: ~5-8g
- Vegetables with dinner: ~5-8g
- Legumes or whole grains at a meal: ~5-8g
- Nuts as snack: ~3g

## Key Points

- High protein diets can cause constipation — fiber helps counteract this
- On a bulk with high calorie intake, getting 30g+ is easy with whole foods
- Increase fiber intake gradually to avoid digestive discomfort
- Drink plenty of water with high-fiber foods
- Vegetables are low-calorie volume-fillers — great for micronutrients too
