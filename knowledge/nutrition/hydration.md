# Hydration Guidelines

## Role

- Temperature regulation
- Joint lubrication
- Nutrient transport
- Performance — even 2% dehydration impairs strength and endurance
- Recovery — hydration aids muscle repair

## Daily Target

| Context | Amount | Notes |
|---------|--------|-------|
| General (sedentary) | 2-2.5L | |
| Active (training) | 3-4L | Includes water from food and drinks |
| Current user target | 3L+ | Active, training 5-6 days/week |

## Timing

| Time | Amount | Purpose |
|------|--------|---------|
| Wake up | 500ml | Rehydrate after sleep |
| Throughout day | 2L+ | Sip consistently |
| Pre-workout (1-2h) | 500ml | Ensure hydration before training |
| During workout | 500-750ml | Per session, sip between sets |
| Post-workout | 500ml+ | Replenish sweat losses |

## During Workout

- Drink 200-300ml every 15-20 minutes during training
- If training >60 min or in hot conditions, consider electrolyte supplementation
- Thirst is not a reliable indicator — drink on a schedule

## Signs of Dehydration

- Dark urine
- Dry mouth
- Headache
- Fatigue
- Decreased performance
- Muscle cramps

## Key Points

- Thirst = already dehydrated. Drink proactively.
- Caffeine is not significantly dehydrating at moderate doses
- Alcohol is dehydrating — compensate with extra water
- Urine colour is the simplest hydration check (pale yellow = good)
- On a bulk with high protein intake, extra water helps kidney function
