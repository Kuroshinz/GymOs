# Fat Guidelines

## Role

- Hormone production (testosterone, etc.)
- Absorption of fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K)
- Cell membrane integrity
- Nervous system function

## Daily Target

| Context | Amount | Notes |
|---------|--------|-------|
| Absolute minimum | 0.5 g/kg (32g for user) | Hormone health floor |
| Recommended range | 0.8-1.0 g/kg (50-63g for user) | General health |
| Current user target | 60-80g | On a lean bulk |
| Never go below | 60g | Hormonal health priority |

## Sources

### Unsaturated (prioritise)
- Avocado (20g per 100g)
- Extra virgin olive oil
- Nuts (almonds, walnuts)
- Seeds (chia, flax, pumpkin)
- Fatty fish (salmon, mackerel, sardines)
- Nut butters

### Saturated (moderate)
- Eggs
- Red meat (lean cuts)
- Dairy (full fat in moderation)
- Coconut oil

### Avoid
- Trans fats
- Hydrogenated oils
- Deep fried foods

## Key Points

- Fat is essential for testosterone production — very low fat diets impair hormone function
- On a bulk, there's room for dietary fat without compromising carb intake
- Prioritise unsaturated fats from whole food sources
- 60g minimum is non-negotiable for the user's hormone health and energy
- Fat has 9 calories/g — calorie-dense, easy to overconsume
