# Supplement Guide (Evidence-Based)

## Tier 1 — Strong Evidence

| Supplement | Dosage | Timing | Benefit |
|------------|--------|--------|---------|
| Whey Protein | 20–40g | Post-workout + any meal | Convenient protein source |
| Creatine Monohydrate | 5g daily | Any (consistent) | Strength, power, muscle gains |
| Caffeine | 2–6mg/kg | 30–60 min pre-workout | Focus, strength, endurance |
| Vitamin D | 2000–5000 IU | With fatty meal | Bone health, immune, mood |
| Omega-3 (EPA/DHA) | 2–3g total | With fatty meal | Inflammation, heart, brain |

## Tier 2 — Good Evidence

| Supplement | Dosage | Timing | Benefit |
|------------|--------|--------|---------|
| Beta-Alanine | 3–6g split | Any | pH buffering, endurance |
| Citrulline Malate | 6–8g | 45–60 min pre | Blood flow, pumps |
| Magnesium | 200–400mg | Before bed | Sleep, recovery, cramps |
| Zinc | 15–30mg | With food | Testosterone, immune |
| Ashwagandha | 300–600mg | Morning or split | Stress, cortisol, recovery |

## Tier 3 — Emerging Evidence

| Supplement | Dosage | Benefit |
|------------|--------|---------|
| HMB | 1.5–3g | Muscle retention in deficit |
| L-Carnitine | 500–2000mg | Recovery, fat oxidation |
| Betaine | 2.5g | Power output |
| Taurine | 1–3g | Recovery, heart health |

## Not Recommended

- BCAA (if protein intake adequate)
- Testosterone boosters (over-the-counter are ineffective)
- Fat burners (mostly stimulants + unproven herbs)
- Glutamine (no benefit if protein adequate)
