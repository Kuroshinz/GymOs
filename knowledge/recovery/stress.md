# Stress Management

## Impact on Training

Stress directly affects:
- **Recovery ability** — high stress impairs muscle repair
- **Performance** — stress reduces strength, endurance, and focus
- **Hormones** — chronic stress elevates cortisol, which is catabolic
- **Sleep quality** — stress is the #1 sleep disruptor
- **Appetite** — stress can reduce or increase food intake
- **Motivation** — high stress reduces training adherence

## Stress Types Affecting Training

| Type | Source | Management |
|------|--------|------------|
| Physical | Training volume, intensity, frequency | Deloading, periodisation |
| Mental | Work, relationships, life | Meditation, relaxation |
| Environmental | Sleep deprivation, poor nutrition, illness | Prioritise basics |
| Recovery | Inadequate sleep or nutrition | Fix the inputs |

## Signs of Excessive Stress

- Persistent fatigue despite adequate sleep
- Decreased performance (weights feel heavier)
- Elevated resting heart rate
- Poor sleep quality
- Loss of appetite or binge eating
- Irritability, low motivation
- Frequent illness

## Mitigation Strategies

1. **Deload** — reduce training volume by 40-60%
2. **Sleep hygiene** — prioritize 7-9 hours
3. **Nutrition** — adequate calories, protein, micronutrients
4. **Relaxation** — deep breathing, meditation, walking
5. **Reduce non-training stressors** where possible
6. **Listen to your body** — skip a session if truly exhausted
