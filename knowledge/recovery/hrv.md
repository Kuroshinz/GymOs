# Heart Rate Variability (HRV)

## What is HRV

The variation in time between heartbeats. Higher HRV = more adaptable nervous system = better recovery. Lower HRV = stress/fatigue accumulation.

## HRV Ranges (RMSSD)

| Age | Excellent | Good | Fair | Poor |
|-----|-----------|------|------|------|
| 18–25 | >80 | 60–80 | 40–60 | <40 |
| 25–35 | >70 | 50–70 | 35–50 | <35 |
| 35–45 | >60 | 45–60 | 30–45 | <30 |
| 45–55 | >50 | 35–50 | 25–35 | <25 |
| 55+ | >40 | 30–40 | 20–30 | <20 |

## How to Track

- Measure every morning upon waking (same time)
- Before getting out of bed, after bathroom break
- Use chest strap (more accurate than wrist/ring)
- Take 3–5 min reading seated or lying
- Log rolling 7-day average (not single day)

## Training Adjustments Based on HRV

| HRV vs Baseline | Interpretation | Training Adjustment |
|----------------|---------------|-------------------|
| Normal (±10%) | Recovered well | Normal training |
| Slightly low (-10 to -20%) | Mild fatigue | Reduce volume/intensity 10–20% |
| Very low (-20%+) | Significant stress | Take rest day or active recovery |
| High (+10%+) | Supercompensated | Can push harder |

## Factors That Lower HRV

- Poor sleep (<6 hours)
- Alcohol (lowers for 48–72 hours)
- Overtraining
- Stress (work, life)
- Dehydration
- Illness (infection)
- High caffeine intake late in day

## Factors That Improve HRV

- Consistent sleep schedule
- Alcohol reduction
- Regular moderate cardio
- Stress management (meditation, breathing)
- Proper hydration
- Consistent meal timing
