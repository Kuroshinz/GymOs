# Sleep for Recovery

## Core Guidelines

| Aspect | Recommendation |
|--------|---------------|
| Duration | 7–9 hours per night |
| Consistency | Same bedtime + wake time (±30 min) |
| Temperature | 18–20°C (65–68°F) |
| Darkness | Complete darkness, blackout curtains |
| Noise | Quiet or white noise |

## Sleep Hygiene

### 2–3 hours before bed
- No heavy meals (light snack okay)
- No alcohol (disrupts REM)
- No intense exercise

### 1 hour before bed
- No screens (blue light disrupts melatonin)
- Dim lights
- No caffeine (half-life 5–6 hours)
- Cool-down routine: reading, stretching, meditation

### Bedroom setup
- Cool, dark, quiet
- Mattress replaced every 7–10 years
- No phone in bedroom

## Sleep and Performance

| Effect of Sleep Debt | Impact |
|----------------------|--------|
| <6 hours for 1 night | 15–20% strength decrease |
| <6 hours for 3+ nights | Cortisol up, testosterone down |
| Chronic <7 hours | Increased injury risk, slower learning |
| Good sleep post-learning | 20–30% better skill retention |

## Napping

| Type | Duration | Benefit |
|------|----------|---------|
| Power nap | 10–20 min | Alertness boost |
| Full cycle | 90 min | Full recovery (rarely needed) |

Avoid naps after 3 PM (disrupts night sleep).
