# Stretching Protocols

## Dynamic Stretching (Pre-Workout)

Purpose: Increase range of motion, activate nervous system, reduce injury risk.

| Exercise | Sets | Reps | Target |
|----------|------|------|--------|
| Leg swings | 1 | 10/leg | Hamstrings, hip flexors |
| Arm circles | 1 | 10 each | Shoulders |
| Hip circles | 1 | 10 each | Hips |
| Cat-cow | 1 | 10 | Spine |
| World's greatest stretch | 1 | 5/side | Full body |
| Inchworm | 1 | 8 | Hamstrings, core |
| Banded pull-apart | 2 | 15 | Shoulder health |
| Banded hip distraction | 1 | 10/side | Hips |

## Static Stretching (Post-Workout or Separate Session)

Purpose: Improve flexibility, reduce muscle tension, cool down.

| Muscle | Stretch | Hold Duration |
|--------|---------|---------------|
| Hamstrings | Seated forward fold | 30–60s |
| Quads | Standing quad stretch | 30–60s/side |
| Chest | Doorway stretch | 30–60s/side |
| Lats | Overhead lat stretch | 30–60s/side |
| Glutes | Figure-4 stretch | 30–60s/side |
| Hip flexors | Half-kneeling hip flexor | 30–60s/side |
| Calves | Downward dog | 30–60s |
| Lower back | Child's pose | 60–90s |

## Frequency

- Dynamic stretching: Before every training session
- Static stretching: After training or on rest days
- Dedicated mobility session: 1–2x per week on rest days
