# Fatigue Management

## Types of Fatigue

| Type | Description | Duration | Recovery |
|------|-------------|----------|----------|
| Acute (session) | Immediate post-workout fatigue | Hours | Rest, food, hydration |
| Cumulative (weekly) | Build-up over training week | Days | Rest days, sleep |
| Systemic (mesocycle) | Accumulated over weeks of training | Weeks | Deload week |
| Overtraining syndrome | Chronic, severe fatigue | Months | Extended break |

## Fatigue Sources

### Neural Fatigue (CNS)
- Heavy compounds (squats, deadlifts, heavy presses)
- High RPE sets (RPE 9-10)
- Explosive/ballistic movements
- Novel or complex exercises

### Metabolic Fatigue
- High rep sets (15+)
- Short rest periods (<60s)
- High volume sessions
- Isolation work

### Systemic Fatigue
- Combined training + life stress
- Poor sleep over multiple nights
- Caloric deficit (not relevant during bulk)
- Illness

## Managing Fatigue

| Strategy | When | What |
|----------|------|------|
| Rest days | Weekly | 1-2 full rest days per week |
| Deload | Every 6-8 weeks | 40-60% volume reduction |
| Session order | Within session | Compounds first, isolation last |
| Exercise order | Within week | Hardest sessions after rest days |
| RPE management | Every set | Stay 1-3 reps from failure |
| Sleep | Daily | 7-9 hours, consistent schedule |

## Fatigue Monitoring

- **Subjective:** "How do you feel today?" (1-5 scale)
- **Performance:** Are weights feeling heavier than expected?
- **Sleep:** Quality and duration tracking
- **HRV:** Optional, tracks autonomic recovery
- **Readiness:** Composite score based on sleep, fatigue, soreness
