# Range of Motion — Evidence Summary

## Summary

Full range of motion (ROM) produces superior hypertrophy compared to partial ROM, particularly through the **lengthened** (stretched) portion of the movement.

The emerging evidence strongly favors emphasising the stretched position for maximal muscle growth.

## Key Takeaways

- Full ROM produces greater hypertrophy than partial ROM
- The lengthened (stretched) portion of the movement may be the MOST important for growth
- Partial ROM at the shortened position (lockout) is the LEAST effective for growth
- Exceptions: some exercises benefit from partials (e.g., high partials for specific overload)
- Stretch-mediated hypertrophy is a real phenomenon
- Going too deep (beyond comfortable ROM) can increase injury risk — use controlled, comfortable full ROM

## Practical Application

- Use full ROM for ALL exercises (unless contraindicated by injury)
- Emphasise the stretched position (pause at bottom of bench, full stretch at bottom of curl)
- For compounds: touch chest on bench, parallel on squat, full hang on pull-ups
- For isolation: full stretch at the bottom (incline curls, dumbbell flyes, lateral raises)
- Never sacrifice load for partial ROM (cheating the stretch)

## Exercise Examples

| Exercise | Full ROM | Partial ROM (avoid) |
|----------|----------|---------------------|
| Bench press | Touch chest, full lockout | Half reps, spotter lifting bar off chest |
| Pull-up | Full hang to chin over bar | Not going to dead hang to start |
| Dumbbell curl | Full stretch to full contraction at top | Not extending fully at bottom |
| Leg press | Full bend to near lockout | 90° quarter squats |
| Lateral raise | Dumbbells to sides alongside hips | Stopping at parallel |
