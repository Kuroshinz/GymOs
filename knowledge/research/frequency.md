# Training Frequency — Evidence Summary

## Summary

Frequency refers to how often a muscle group is trained per week.

For hypertrophy, higher frequency (2-4× per week) is generally superior to lower frequency (1× per week), primarily because it allows more volume per session without excessive per-session fatigue.

## Key Takeaways

- 2× per week per muscle group is superior to 1× per week for hypertrophy
- 3× per week may be slightly better than 2×, but the difference is small
- Frequency matters most when total volume is high (more sets = more sessions needed)
- Frequency matters less when volume is equated (same total sets, more sessions)
- Higher frequency allows for better technique practice
- Higher frequency means less per-session fatigue and better recovery

## Frequency Recommendations

| Muscle Group | Minimum | Optimal | Notes |
|-------------|---------|---------|-------|
| Chest | 2×/week | 2-3× | Responds well to higher frequency |
| Back | 2×/week | 2-3× | Can handle high volume across sessions |
| Shoulders | 2×/week | 2-4× | Side/rear delts recover fast |
| Arms | 2×/week | 2-4× | Small muscles, fast recovery |
| Legs | 1.5-2×/week | 2× | High systemic fatigue from compounds |

## Practical Application

PPL-UL trains each muscle group ~2× per 10-day cycle:
- Push A / Push B = chest, shoulders, triceps 2×
- Pull A / Pull B = back, biceps, rear delts 2×
- Legs A / Legs B = quads, hamstrings, glutes 2×
- Upper A / Upper B = additional upper body stimulus
