# Training Volume — Evidence Summary

## Summary

Volume (number of challenging working sets per muscle group per week) shows a clear dose-response relationship with hypertrophy.

More sets = more growth, up to a point. Beyond that point, additional sets yield diminishing returns and eventually negative returns (due to incomplete recovery).

## Key Takeaways

- There is a positive dose-response between volume and hypertrophy
- Going from 1 set to 5 sets per week produces significant gains
- Going from 5 to 10 sets adds additional gains
- 10-20 sets per muscle group per week appears optimal
- Beyond 20+ sets, additional gains are minimal for most people
- More volume requires more recovery (sleep, nutrition, deloads)
- Volume should be periodised (build up, deload, repeat)
- Per session: 10-20 working sets optimal

## Evidence Landmarks

| Weekly Sets | Effect |
|-------------|--------|
| 1-4 | Minimal growth (better than nothing) |
| 5-9 | Moderate growth |
| 10-14 | Good growth (minimum effective dose) |
| 15-19 | Optimal growth (sweet spot) |
| 20+ | Diminishing returns, increased recovery demands |

## Practical Application

- Start at 12-14 weekly sets per muscle group
- Progress by adding 1-2 sets per muscle group when progress stalls
- Max out at 18-20 sets before deload
- After deload, reset to 12-14 sets and build up again
- Prioritise volume for focus muscles (shoulders, back width, arms)
