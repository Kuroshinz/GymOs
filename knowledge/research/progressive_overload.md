# Progressive Overload — Evidence Summary

## Summary

Progressive overload is the fundamental principle underlying all muscle growth. The body adapts to a stimulus by becoming stronger and/or larger; to continue growing, the stimulus must progressively increase over time.

Without progressive overload, there is no hypertrophy.

## Key Takeaways

- Progressive overload is REQUIRED for continued muscle growth
- Overload can be achieved through: increased weight, increased reps, increased sets, decreased rest, increased frequency, or improved technique
- For hypertrophy, **double progression** (reps first, then weight) is the most sustainable method for long-term growth
- Linear progression works for beginners but plateaus quickly
- The principle applies across all variables: load, volume, frequency, density, and exercise selection
- Tracking every session is essential to ensure overload is actually occurring

## Methods of Progressive Overload

| Method | Best For | Sustainability |
|--------|----------|----------------|
| Increase weight | All phases | Limited (can't add weight forever) |
| Increase reps | Hypertrophy | High (can always try for one more rep) |
| Increase sets | Volume accumulation | High |
| Decrease rest | Density training | Moderate |
| Improve technique | Beginners | Limited (diminishes with skill) |
| Increase frequency | Lagging muscles | Moderate |

## Double Progression Protocol

1. Select a weight where you can perform the LOWER end of the rep range (e.g., 8 reps of 8-12)
2. Each session, try to add 1+ rep while maintaining the same weight
3. Once you hit the UPPER end of the rep range (12 reps), increase weight by 1.25-2.5 kg
4. You will drop back to the lower rep range (8 reps with new weight)
5. Repeat
