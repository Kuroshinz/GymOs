# Strength Periodization

## Key Principles

- **Progressive overload** is the fundamental driver of strength gains
- Periodization helps manage fatigue and prevent plateaus
- Linear periodization works for beginners, more complex models for advanced

## Periodization Models

| Model | Description | Best For |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| Linear | Increase weight weekly, decrease reps | Beginners (first 6–12 months) |
| Undulating | Vary reps/load each session | Intermediate |
| Block | 3–6 week blocks focused on one quality | Advanced |
| Conjugate | Train multiple qualities simultaneously | Advanced athletes |

## Rep Ranges for Strength

| Goal | Reps | % 1RM | RPE |
|------|------|-------|-----|
| Maximal strength | 1–5 | 85–100% | 9–10 |
| Strength + size | 5–8 | 75–85% | 7–9 |
| Strength endurance | 8–12 | 65–75% | 6–8 |

## Key Findings

- Heavy loads (85%+) are superior for maximal strength
- Multiple sets are superior to single sets
- 2–3 min rest is optimal for strength development
- Frequency: 2–3x per week per lift for optimal progress
