# Recovery — Evidence Summary

## Summary

Recovery is the process by which muscle tissue repairs and grows stronger after training. Training stimulates the growth signal; recovery IS the growth.

No amount of perfect training can overcome poor recovery.

## Key Takeaways

- Muscle protein synthesis (MPS) peaks 24-48 hours after training and remains elevated for up to 72 hours
- Sleep is the single most important recovery factor (7-9 hours minimum)
- Protein intake directly supports recovery and MPS
- Stress elevates cortisol, which is catabolic and impairs recovery
- Active recovery (light movement, walking) speeds clearance of metabolic waste
- Deload weeks are essential for long-term progress (prevent overtraining)
- Individual recovery capacity varies — track and adjust

## Recovery Factors

| Factor | Impact | Optimal |
|--------|--------|---------|
| Sleep | CRITICAL | 7-9 hours, consistent schedule |
| Protein intake | HIGH | 1.6-2.2 g/kg daily |
| Calorie balance | HIGH | Surplus for growth |
| Stress management | MODERATE | Low chronic stress |
| Hydration | MODERATE | 3-4L daily for active individuals |
| Active recovery | MODERATE | Light walking on rest days |
| Deload | HIGH (long-term) | Every 6-8 weeks |

## Recovery Timeline

| Timeframe | Process |
|-----------|---------|
| 0-4 hours post-workout | Glycogen replenishment, acute inflammation |
| 4-24 hours | MPS begins, metabolic waste clearance |
| 24-48 hours | Peak MPS, muscle repair |
| 48-72 hours | Continued repair, adaptation |
| 3-7 days | Full recovery (varies by intensity/volume) |
| 6-8 weeks | System fatigue builds — deload needed |
