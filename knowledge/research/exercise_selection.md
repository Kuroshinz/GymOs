# Exercise Selection — Evidence Summary

## Summary

Exercise selection significantly impacts hypertrophy outcomes. The best exercises for growth are those that:
1. Produce high mechanical tension in the target muscle
2. Allow for progressive overload
3. Provide a good stretch in the lengthened position
4. Are sustainable long-term (joint-friendly, can be trained hard)

## Key Takeaways

- Compound exercises are NOT inherently superior to isolation for hypertrophy (both have their place)
- Compounds should come FIRST in a session (most demanding, systemic stimulus)
- Isolation exercises allow for more targeted volume on specific muscles
- Exercise variation is useful to target different regions of a muscle
- Machine exercises are NOT inferior to free weights for hypertrophy (and may be safer)
- Stretch-position exercises (e.g., incline curls, dumbbell flyes) show emerging evidence for superiority
- Exercise selection should prioritise the user's weak points / focus muscles

## Compound vs Isolation

| | Compounds | Isolation |
|--|-----------|-----------|
| Systemic stimulus | High | Low |
| Muscle targeting | Multiple muscles | Single muscle |
| CNS fatigue | High | Low |
| Progressive overload | Easy (big weight jumps) | Harder (smaller jumps) |
| Safety | Higher injury risk if poor form | Safer |
| When to use | First in session | After compounds |
| Example focus | Chest = Incline press | Side delt = Lateral raises |

## Exercise Selection Principles

1. **Priority first:** Choose exercises that target the user's focus muscles (shoulders, upper chest, back width, arms)
2. **Variety within reason:** Change exercise selection every 4-8 weeks, not every session
3. **Compound + isolation:** Each muscle group needs at least one compound + one isolation
4. **Stretch emphasis:** Where possible, choose variations that load the lengthened position
5. **Sustainability:** Avoid exercises that cause joint pain (find alternatives)
6. **Progression:** The exercise must allow for consistent weight/reps increases
