# Hypertrophy — Evidence Summary

## Summary

Muscle hypertrophy (growth) is driven by three primary mechanisms:
1. **Mechanical tension** — the force produced by and applied to muscle during contraction
2. **Metabolic stress** — accumulation of metabolites from anaerobic work
3. **Muscle damage** — micro-trauma to muscle fibres from eccentric contractions

All effective hypertrophy training programmes manipulate these factors through appropriate load, volume, and exercise selection.

## Key Takeaways

- Mechanical tension is the PRIMARY driver of hypertrophy
- Training within 6-15 reps (30-80% 1RM) produces similar hypertrophy when sets are taken close to failure
- Volume (number of challenging sets) is the most manipulable variable for hypertrophy
- Going to failure is NOT required for growth; stopping 1-3 reps short is sufficient
- Training each muscle group 2× per week (at least) is superior to 1× per week
- Full range of motion produces greater hypertrophy than partial ROM (especially at lengthened position)

## Practical Application

| Variable | Recommendation |
|----------|---------------|
| Rep range | 6-15 reps (all effective, vary for preference) |
| Proximity to failure | 1-3 RIR for most sets |
| Frequency | 2× per week per muscle group (minimum) |
| Volume | 10-20 weekly sets per muscle group |
| ROM | Full ROM, emphasis on lengthened position |
| Rest | 60-90 seconds for hypertrophy |
