# Nutrition for Hypertrophy — Evidence Summary

## Summary

Nutrition provides the raw materials for muscle growth. Without adequate calories and protein, no amount of training will produce significant hypertrophy.

## Key Takeaways

**Calories:** A modest calorie surplus (300-500 kcal above maintenance) is optimal for muscle gain with minimal fat gain.

**Protein:** 1.6-2.2 g/kg/day is the evidence-based range for muscle gain. Higher intakes (up to 3.0 g/kg) may benefit during a cut.

**Carbohydrates:** Primary fuel for high-intensity training. Adequate intake spares protein and supports performance.

**Fats:** Minimum 0.5-1.0 g/kg/day for hormonal health. Low fat diets impair testosterone production.

**Timing:** Total daily intake matters more than timing. Meeting daily targets consistently is the priority.

## Evidence-Based Targets

| Nutrient | Recommendation | Evidence Level |
|----------|---------------|----------------|
| Calorie surplus | 300-500 kcal/day | Strong |
| Protein | 1.6-2.2 g/kg/day | Strong |
| Fat minimum | 0.5-1.0 g/kg/day | Strong |
| Pre/post carbs | 40-60g each | Moderate |
| Meal frequency | 3-5 meals/day | Weak (individual preference) |

## Supplement Evidence

| Supplement | Evidence | Recommendation |
|-----------|----------|----------------|
| Creatine monohydrate | Strong — increases strength and lean mass | 5g/day |
| Whey protein | Strong — convenient protein source | As needed for protein target |
| Caffeine | Strong — improves performance | Pre-workout |
| Beta-alanine | Moderate — buffers lactate | Optional |
| Vitamin D3 | Strong if deficient | Daily supplementation |
| BCAAs | Weak — unnecessary if protein is adequate | Not recommended |
