# Hypertrophy Mechanisms

## Key Mechanisms

1. **Mechanical Tension**: Primary driver of muscle growth
   - Loaded muscle under stretch
   - Time under tension at moderate-to-high loads

2. **Metabolic Stress**: Secondary driver
   - Pump, burn, cell swelling
   - Higher reps (12–20) with short rest

3. **Muscle Damage**: Contributes to remodeling
   - Eccentric emphasis
   - Novel exercises

## Volume Recommendations

| Level | Sets per muscle per week |
|-------|-------------------------|
| Minimum effective | 5–8 |
| Optimal for most | 10–15 |
| Maximum recoverable | 15–20 |
| Junk volume | >20 (diminishing returns + fatigue) |

## Intensity (Load) for Hypertrophy

- Effective range: 30–85% 1RM
- Optimal range: 60–80% 1RM (6–20 reps)
- All ranges can work if taken close to failure

## Rest Intervals

- Compound exercises: 2–3 min
- Isolation exercises: 60–90s
- High-rep metabolic work: 30–60s
