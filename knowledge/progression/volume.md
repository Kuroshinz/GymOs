# Training Volume

## Definition

Total amount of work performed. For hypertrophy, measured as:

- **Working sets per muscle group per week** (primary metric)
- Reps × weight × sets (total volume load — secondary)

## Volume Landmarks

| Muscle Group | Min (maintenance) | Optimal (growth) | Max (recovery limit) |
|-------------|-------------------|------------------|---------------------|
| Chest | 8 | 14-18 | 22 |
| Back | 10 | 16-20 | 25 |
| Shoulders (total) | 8 | 14-18 | 22 |
| Side Delts (iso) | 4 | 8-12 | 16 |
| Rear Delts (iso) | 3 | 6-8 | 12 |
| Biceps | 6 | 12-16 | 20 |
| Triceps | 6 | 12-16 | 20 |
| Quads | 6 | 12-16 | 20 |
| Hamstrings/Glutes | 6 | 10-14 | 18 |
| Calves | 4 | 8-12 | 16 |
| Abs | 4 | 8-12 | 16 |

## Per Session

- 10-20 working sets per session (excluding warm-up)
- Beyond 20 sets per session shows diminishing returns
- 15-20 sets is the "sweet spot" for most sessions

## Volume Progression

1. Add 1-2 sets per muscle group when progress stalls
2. Max out volume within the optimal range
3. Deload when approaching maximum volume
4. After deload, return to ~60-70% of max volume and build up again

## What Counts as a "Working Set"

- Sets taken to within 1-3 reps of failure (RPE 7+)
- Warm-up sets do NOT count
- Technical failure sometimes acceptable for isolation (1-2 final sets)
