# Deload Rules

## What is a Deload?

A planned reduction in training volume/intensity to allow recovery and supercompensation.

## When to Deload

| Scenario | Action |
|----------|--------|
| Every 4–6 weeks | Schedule 1 week deload |
| Stalled progression for 2+ weeks | Deload and reset |
| Fatigue accumulating (poor sleep, mood, motivation) | Immediate deload |
| After competition or peak week | 1–2 week deload |

## How to Deload

| Variable | Adjustment |
|----------|-----------|
| Volume | Reduce by 40–60% (sets, not reps) |
| Intensity | Reduce by 10–20% (lighter weight) |
| Frequency | Keep same (don't skip sessions) |
| RPE | Stay at 4–6, not 8–10 |

## What NOT to Do During Deload

- Don't stop training completely (deconditioning)
- Don't test maxes (you're deloaded, not primed)
- Don't add new exercises (stick to staples)

## After Deload

- Resume at the same weight you left off (or slightly lower)
- Expect to be a bit rusty — strength returns within 1–2 weeks
- Reset rep ranges to bottom of the range
