# Linear Progression

## Overview

The simplest form of progressive overload: add weight to the bar every session (or every week).

Best for: beginners, early intermediates, and the first phase of any new program.

## How It Works

```
Session 1: Bench Press — 40 kg × 8, 8, 8
Session 2: Bench Press — 42.5 kg × 8, 8, 8
Session 3: Bench Press — 45 kg × 8, 8, 8
```

## When to Use

- First 4-8 weeks of a new exercise or program
- When the user is undershooting their capacity
- After a deload (short-term linear rebound)
- Never sustainable long-term (diminishing returns)

## Limitations

- Not sustainable beyond 4-12 weeks
- Leads to plateaus and burnout if used indefinitely
- Poor for hypertrophy (low reps, low volume accumulation)
- Best replaced with double progression for long-term hypertrophy

## Application in GymOS

Linear progression is used:
- As the initial phase when starting a new exercise (find working weight)
- During the rebound week after a deload
- NOT as the primary hypertrophy progression method (use double progression)
