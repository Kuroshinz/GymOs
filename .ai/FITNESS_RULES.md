# GymOS — Hypertrophy Training Rules

## Overview

These rules define how GymOS approaches hypertrophy training. Every workout recommendation, progression decision, and exercise selection flows from these principles.

---

## 1. Progressive Overload

The fundamental driver of muscle growth. Without progressive overload, there is no hypertrophy.

### Definition
Progressive overload means systematically increasing the training stimulus over time to force the muscles to adapt and grow.

### Application
- Each workout, aim to do MORE than last time
- "More" means: more weight, more reps, or more sets
- Track everything to ensure overload is actually happening
- Plateau = time to change the variable or deload

---

## 2. Double Progression

The primary progression method for hypertrophy.

### How It Works

**Phase 1 — Reps first:**
1. Pick a weight you can do for the lower end of the rep range (e.g., 8 reps of 8-12)
2. Each session, try to add 1+ rep while keeping the same weight
3. Continue until you hit the top of the rep range (e.g., 12 reps)

**Phase 2 — Weight next:**
1. Increase the weight by the smallest increment (typically 1.25-2.5 kg)
2. You will drop back to the lower end of the rep range (e.g., 8 reps)
3. Repeat Phase 1

### Example

| Session | Weight | Reps | Phase |
|---------|--------|------|-------|
| 1 | 40 kg | 8 | Reps ascending |
| 2 | 40 kg | 9 | Reps ascending |
| 3 | 40 kg | 10 | Reps ascending |
| 4 | 40 kg | 11 | Reps ascending |
| 5 | 40 kg | 12 | Reps ascending |
| 6 | 42.5 kg | 8 | Weight increase |
| 7 | 42.5 kg | 9 | Reps ascending (repeat) |

### Why Double Progression?
- More volume accumulation than 5×5 strength schemes
- Better hypertrophy stimulus from higher reps
- Clear, measurable progress every session
- Built-in deload mechanism (weight jumps naturally reduce reps)

|-------------|---------|---------|---------|

| Shoulders (total) | 8 | 14-18 | 22 |
| Side Delts (isolation) | 4 | 8-12 | 16 |
| Rear Delts (isolation) | 3 | 6-8 | 12 |
| Biceps | 6 | 12-16 | 20 |
| Triceps | 6 | 12-16 | 20 |
| Quads | 6 | 12-16 | 20 |
| Hamstrings / Glutes | 6 | 10-14 | 18 |
| Calves | 4 | 8-12 | 16 |
| Abs | 4 | 8-12 | 16 |

### Per-Session Volume
- 10-20 working sets per session (excluding warm-up)
- Technical failure limit: stop 1-2 reps from failure for compounds, closer for isolation

## 4. Training Frequency

### PPL-UL Split

| Day | Session | Focus |
|-----|---------|-------|
| 1 | Push A | Heavy compounds + volume isolations |
| 2 | Pull A | Horizontal pull + vertical pull |
| 3 | Legs A | Quad-dominant |
| 4 | Upper A | Strength focus (heavier, lower reps) |
| 5 | Lower A | Posterior chain focus |
| 6 | Push B | Higher volume, more isolation |
| 7 | Pull B | Width focus (lats + rear delts) |
| 8 | Legs B | Hamstring-dominant |
| 9 | Upper B | Volume focus (higher reps, more sets) |
| 10 | Lower B | Quad + glute volume |

### Why PPL-UL?
- Hits every muscle group 2× per 10-day cycle
- Higher frequency = more protein synthesis stimulation
- Upper/Lower days add extra stimulus to lagging upper body
- Flexible scheduling — can take rest days as needed

## 5. Recovery

### Training to Failure

| Exercise Type | Approach |
|--------------|----------|
| Compound (squat, bench, row, OHP) | Stop 1-2 reps shy of failure |
| Isolation (curls, lateral raises, extensions) | Can go to 0-1 RIR (near failure) |
| Machines (chest press, lat pulldown) | 0-1 RIR acceptable (safer than free weights) |

### Rest Times

| Goal | Rest Time |
|------|-----------|
| Hypertrophy (8-15 reps) | 60-90 seconds |
| Strength (1-5 reps) | 2-4 minutes |
| Isolation / accessories | 45-60 seconds |
| Between warm-up sets | 30-60 seconds |

### Tempo

| Phase | Recommended | Notes |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| Eccentric (lowering) | 2-3 seconds | Controlled, feel the stretch |
| Stretch (bottom) | 1 second brief pause | Don't bounce |
| Concentric (lifting) | 1-2 seconds | Explosive but controlled |
| Contraction (top) | 1 second squeeze | Flex the target muscle |

## 6. Deload

### When to Deload
- After 6-8 weeks of consistent training
- When progress stalls for 2+ consecutive sessions
- When RPE feels higher than expected for given weights
- When sleep, mood, or motivation drops significantly
- When returning from a layoff (start with deload)

### How to Deload
- Reduce volume by 40-60% (half the sets)
- Keep intensity the same (don't reduce weight)
- Keep frequency the same (don't skip sessions)
- Focus on technique and mind-muscle connection

### Deload Week Example

| Normal Week | Deload Week |
|-------------|-------------|
| Exercise: Bench Press | Same exercise |
| 4 sets × 8 reps @ 60 kg | 2 sets × 8 reps @ 60 kg |
| Exercise: Lateral Raise | Same exercise |
| 3 sets × 12 reps @ 8 kg | 2 sets × 12 reps @ 8 kg |

## 7. Exercise Selection

### Prioritised Muscles (User's Focus)

| Priority | Muscle | Why |
|----------|--------|-----|
| 1 | Shoulders (side + rear delts) | Lateral raises, face pulls, OHP — most visible upper body muscle for aesthetic |
| 2 | Upper Chest | Incline press, low-to-high fly — creates the "armour plate" look |
| 3 | Back Width (lats) | Lat pulldowns, pull-ups, wide rows — V-taper is key for aesthetic |
| 4 | Arms (biceps + triceps) | Curls, extensions, hammer curls — proportionate, visible |

### General Principles
- Start session with compound movements (most demanding, best systemic stimulus)
- End session with isolation (target specific muscles, less CNS fatigue)
- Prefer free weights for compounds, machines for isolation
- Include at least one vertical pull and one horizontal pull per back session
- Include at least one overhead press variation for shoulders
- Stretch-position exercises first (lengthened partials emerging evidence)

## 8. Rep Ranges

| Goal | Rep Range | Best For |
|------|-----------|----------|
| Strength | 1-5 | Neural adaptation, heavy compounds |
| Hypertrophy | 8-15 | Primary range for muscle growth |
| Pump / Endurance | 15-25 | Isolation, warm-up, blood flow |
| Warm-up | 5-10 ascending | Prepare for working weight |

### Hypertrophy Range Guidelines
- **Lower body compounds:** 8-12 reps
- **Upper body compounds:** 8-12 reps
- **Isolation upper:** 10-15 reps
- **Isolation lower:** 12-20 reps
- **Side/rear delts:** 12-20 reps (respond better to higher reps)
- **Calves:** 12-20 reps (slow-twitch dominant)
- **Abs:** 12-20 reps

---

## 9. Warm-Up Protocol

Before every session:
1. **5-10 minutes** — light cardio (walking, bike, rower) to increase core temperature
2. **Dynamic stretching** — arm circles, leg swings, hip rotations, cat-cow
3. **Specific warm-up** — for each exercise:
   - Bar only × 8-10 reps
   - 40-50% working weight × 5 reps
   - 60-70% working weight × 3 reps
   - 80-90% working weight × 1 rep (optional for heavier compounds)
4. **Activation** — band pull-aparts before pressing, face pulls for shoulder health

---

## 10. Cooldown

After every session:
1. **Light cardio** — 5 minutes walking or very light cycling
2. **Static stretching** — hold each stretch 15-30 seconds for worked muscle groups
3. **Key stretches:**
   - Chest: doorway stretch
   - Lats: overhead reach to side
   - Shoulders: cross-body arm stretch
   - Quads: standing quad stretch
   - Hamstrings: seated or standing hamstring stretch

## 11. Nutrition for Lean Bulk

### Calorie Target
- **Start:** ~2800 kcal/day
- **Adjust:** +100 kcal if no weight gain in 2 weeks; -100 kcal if gaining >0.5 kg/week
- **Rate:** 0.25-0.5 kg per week

### Protein
- **Minimum:** 140g/day (2.2 g/kg bodyweight)
- **Optimal:** 150-170g/day
- **Source:** 40% from food, 60% from shakes as needed

### Carbohydrates
- **Pre-workout (1-2 hours before):** 40-60g fast-digesting carbs
- **Post-workout:** 40-60g carbs + protein
- **Total:** 300-400g/day (fill remaining calories after protein/fat)

### Fats
- **Minimum:** 60g/day (hormone health)
- **Range:** 60-80g/day
- **Focus:** Unsaturated fats (avocado, nuts, olive oil, fatty fish)

### Hydration
- **Minimum:** 3L water/day
- **During workout:** 500-750ml per session

## 12. RPE / RIR Reference

| RPE | Description | Reps in Reserve | Effort |
|-----|-------------|-----------------|--------|
| 10 | Maximum effort, cannot do another rep | 0 RIR | Absolute failure |
| 9 | Very hard, could do 1 more rep | 1 RIR | Heavy, but safe |
| 8 | Hard, could do 2 more reps | 2 RIR | Good intensity |
| 7 | Somewhat hard, 3 reps left | 3 RIR | Moderate |
| 6 | Manageable, 4+ reps left | 4+ RIR | Light working set |
| 5 | Warm-up weight | ∞ | Not taxing |

### RPE Targets
- **Compounds (bench, squat, row, OHP):** RPE 7-8 (leave 2-3 reps in tank)
- **Secondary compounds:** RPE 8 (leave 1-2 reps)
- **Isolation:** RPE 8-9 (leave 1-2 or go to failure on last set)
- **Never train to RPE 10 on compounds (injury risk, CNS fatigue)**

---

## 13. Exercise Order

Within each session:
1. **Compound / multi-joint first** (most demanding, requires most energy)
2. **Secondary compound** 
3. **Isolation / single-joint** (least CNS demand)
4. **Accessories / prehab** (face pulls, band work)

### Example: Push A
1. Incline Barbell Press (compound, upper chest focus)
2. Dumbbell Overhead Press (compound, shoulder focus)
3. Dips (compound, lower chest + triceps)
4. Lateral Raise (isolation, side delt)
5. Tricep Pushdown (isolation)
6. Face Pull (prehab, rear delt)

## 14. Progression Speed

| Exercise Type | Weight Jump | Frequency |
|--------------|-------------|-----------|
| Upper body compounds | 1.25-2.5 kg | Every 2-4 weeks |
| Lower body compounds | 2.5-5 kg | Every 2-4 weeks |
| Isolation | 1.25-2.5 kg | Every 3-6 weeks |
| Machines | 2.5-5 kg | Every 2-4 weeks |

### When to Progress
- Hit top of rep range for ALL sets of an exercise at current weight
- Then add weight and repeat

### When to Reset
- Cannot progress for 4+ weeks on an exercise
- Reset weight by 10-15% and build back up
- Consider swapping exercise variation
