# Periodization Model — GymOS Planning Engine

## Scientific Foundation

The Planning Engine uses **block periodization** as its primary model, with **linear periodization** for strength-focused mesocycles and **undulating periodization** for hypertrophy mesocycles.

### Why Block Periodization?

Block periodization concentrates training stimuli into focused blocks (mesocycles), allowing for:
1. Greater accumulation of specific adaptations
2. Better management of fatigue
3. Clearer progression tracking
4. More precise deload scheduling

## Progression Models

### Double Progression (Hypertrophy)
1. Find weight at target rep range (e.g., 8-12)
2. Add reps until top of range
3. Increase weight 2.5-5%
4. Drop back to bottom of rep range
5. Repeat

### Linear Progression (Strength)
1. Start at 3×5 @ 75%
2. Add 2.5kg per session
3. Deload at first failure
4. Restart at 80% of peak

### Wave Loading (Power)
1. Week 1: 3×3 @ 75%
2. Week 2: 3×2 @ 82%
3. Week 3: 3×1 @ 90%
4. Reset, repeat with +2.5%

## Deload Scheduling

Deloads occur every 4-8 training weeks depending on:
- **Intensity**: Higher intensity = more frequent deloads
- **Volume**: Higher volume = more frequent deloads
- **Recovery Capacity**: Lower capacity = more frequent deloads
- **User Risk Tolerance**: Aggressive = less frequent, Conservative = more frequent

### Deload Prescription
- Volume: 40-60% of peak
- Intensity: 40-50% of 1RM
- RIR: 3+ reps in reserve
- Frequency: Maintain or reduce by 1 session
- Duration: 5-7 days
